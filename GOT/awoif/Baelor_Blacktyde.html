Baelor Blacktyde is Lord of Blacktyde and head of House Blacktyde. Baelor captains the Nightflyer.[2]

Appearance

Baelor is smooth-faced and handsome.[2] He wears a lambswool tunic of black-and-green vairy, and he pins his sable cloak with a silver seven-pointed star.[2]

History

Baelor's father, Lord Blacktyde, died during Greyjoy's Rebellion, and Baelor spent eight years as a hostage in Oldtown. When he came back to the Iron Islands, he was a worshiper of the Faith of the Seven.[2] Balon Greyjoy, Lord of the Iron Islands, sent Tristifer Botley to ward with Baelor after Tris was caught playing with Balon's daughter, Asha Greyjoy.[3]

Recent Events

A Feast for Crows

Lord Baelor stops at Ten Towers to consult with Lord Rodrik Harlaw before making his way to Old Wyk for the kingsmoot.[3] At the kingsmoot, he asks Victarion Greyjoy if he would end the war. Baelor moves on when Victarion rejects the idea.[2] He is one of the first to shout Asha Greyjoy's name at the kingsmoot.[4]

After Euron Greyjoy is raised as King of the Iron Islands at the kingsmoot, Baelor refuses to acknowledge Euron and attempts to return to Blacktyde. Victarion closes Nagga's Cradle with the Iron Fleet, however, and Baelor is taken and delivered to King Euron in chains. As punishment for his refusal to acknowledge his new liege, Baelor is cut by Euron's crew into seven pieces, representing the Faith of the Seven.[1] Euron claims Baelor's sable cloak for himself.[1]
